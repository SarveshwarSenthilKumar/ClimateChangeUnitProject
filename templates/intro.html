Eco-Mobile
Did you Internal Combustion Engine transport accounted for 182 million tons of CO2 emitted into the atmosphere this year? This contributes a lot to climate change, which is causing many horrible effects such as rising sea-levels, increasing temperatures, and so much more. The goal of Eco-Mobile is to convince the members of our society to stray away from big gas-guzzling SUVs to smaller more fuel-efficient vehicles to stop this from happening. If you would like more information on where the statistics are collected from, please scroll down.


This site compares the environmental and economical impacts of different vehicles, this is based off of the national average of each statistic in Canada. For the average number of kilometers driven per year, the statistic was taken from https://www.canadadrives.ca/blog/driving-tips/how-your-annual-kms-per-year-impacts-value-insurance-lease. Meanwhile the cars listed are the most sold vehicles in Canada according to https://www.kbb.ca/news-details/10-most-popular-new-vehicles-in-canada-for-2021-/?ID=179 and some more cars from some notable brands such as Kia and BMW, for which the sales figures come from https://carfigures.com/canada-market-brand/. All the gas mileage statistics for the vehicles were taken from https://fueleconomy.gov/, a division of the EPA (Environmental Protection Agency). The Litre to Carbon Dioxide calculation is according to https://www.nrcan.gc.ca/sites/www.nrcan.gc.ca/files/oee/pdf/transportation/fuel-efficient-technologies/autosmart_factsheet_6_e.pdf#:~:text=Burning%201%20L%20of%20gasoline%20produces%20approximately%202.3,600%20kg%20of%20CO%202%20into%20the%20atmosphere. , an article by Natural Resources Canada, a division of the Canadian Government. Last but not least, all calculations on this site are calculated according to the average price per litre of gas in Ontario, Canada as of October 31st. These statistics are from https://www.caa.ca/gas-prices/.
